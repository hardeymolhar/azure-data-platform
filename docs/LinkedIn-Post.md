Revalidation — LinkedIn Posting Timetable (Mon–Fri)

Timezone: WAT (UTC+1)
Objective: Verify the schedule is optimal for your Nigeria-heavy audience while moving visibility toward Europe & US. Result: Schedule is valid but requires a disciplined A/B validation process and small tactical tweaks to force global overlap.

Summary — what to do and why it matters

Do this: Run the provided timetable for a controlled 4-week experiment while collecting post-level metrics; A/B test the Wednesday global window against a morning window; apply the Dual-Boost engagement pattern daily.
Why it matters: Timing only amplifies good content. Without measurement, you’ll keep repeating best guesses. A structured test converts a timing hypothesis into repeatable reach gains and lets you shift audience composition toward senior/global profiles.

Daily non-post actions (every workday):

First 60 minutes after posting: immediate replies + 3 targeted replies to influencers

16:30–18:00: global commenting session (5–10 meaningful comments)

Validation plan — step-by-step (strict)

Baseline week (Week 0)

Record last 4 weeks of analytics (impressions, unique viewers, % non-local, engagement rate).

If you don’t have export: take screenshots of the Followers & Post analytics dashboard.

Run experiment (Weeks 1–4)

Follow timetable exactly.

On Wednesdays split test across weeks:

Week 1 & 3 → Post at 16:30 WAT (global window).

Week 2 & 4 → Post at 09:00 WAT (control morning window).

Use identical post types for each tested pair (same format + similar length) to isolate time as the variable.

Collect metrics per post (immediately and at 24h, 72h):

Impressions

Unique viewers

Engagements (likes, comments, shares)

Engagement rate = engagements / impressions

% non-local impressions (where available)

Recruiter / senior profiles engaging (manually note)

Decision criteria (end of Week 4)

If Wednesday@16:30 shows ≥10% higher non-local impressions and ≥10% higher engagement rate versus Wednesday@09:00 → keep the 16:30 global window.

If differences are within ±10% → keep the morning slot (favor consistency).

If engagement drops >15% at 16:30 → revert to morning and run alternative global tactic (targeted commenting).

Iterate

If targets met, scale: add one additional global-oriented post every other week at 16:30.

If not met, refine content (tone, hook, CTA) and re-run another 4-week cycle.
